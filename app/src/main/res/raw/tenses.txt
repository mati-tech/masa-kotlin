In English, there are 12 basic tenses, which are formed using different combinations of auxiliary verbs (also known as helping verbs) and main verbs. These tenses are divided into three main categories: simple, continuous (progressive), and perfect. Here's an overview of each tense:


1. Simple Present Tense:
   - Form: Subject + Base Form of Verb (+ Object)
   - Example: She reads books every day.


2. Present Continuous Tense:
   - Form: Subject + "to be" (am/is/are) + Present Participle (-ing form of verb) (+ Object)
   - Example: She is reading** a book right now.


3. Present Perfect Tense:
   - Form: Subject + "to have" (have/has) + Past Participle of Verb (+ Object)
   - Example: She has read many books this year.


4. Present Perfect Continuous Tense:
   - Form: Subject + "to have" (have/has) + Been + Present Participle (-ing form of verb) (+ Object)
   - Example: She has been reading for two hours.


5. Simple Past Tense:
   - Form: Subject + Past Form of Verb (+ Object)
   - Example: She read a book yesterday.


6. Past Continuous Tense:
   - Form: Subject + "to be" (was/were) + Present Participle (-ing form of verb) (+ Object)
   - Example: She was reading a book when the phone rang.


7. Past Perfect Tense:
   - Form: Subject + "to have" (had) + Past Participle of Verb (+ Object)
   - Example: She **had read** all the books before the exam started.


8. Past Perfect Continuous Tense:
   - Form: Subject + "to have" (had) + Been + Present Participle (-ing form of verb) (+ Object)
   - Example: She had been reading for hours when I arrived.


9. Simple Future Tense:
   - Form: Subject + "will" or "shall" + Base Form of Verb (+ Object)
   - Example: She will read a book tomorrow.


10. Future Continuous Tense:
    - Form: Subject + "will be" + Present Participle (-ing form of verb) (+ Object)
    - Example: She will be reading** a book at this time tomorrow.


11. Future Perfect Tense:
    - Form: Subject + "will have" + Past Participle of Verb (+ Object)
    - Example: She **will have read** all the books by next week.


12. Future Perfect Continuous Tense:
    - Form: Subject + "will have been" + Present Participle (-ing form of verb) (+ Object)
    - Example: She will have been reading for three hours by the time you arrive.
